About
About EconomicCrimeHub.org (and me)
An independent, non-commercial resource for learning about economic crime.
Hi — I’m David, and I created EconomicCrimeHub.org entirely as a personal, independent side project.

It began as a straightforward experiment: I wanted to understand what people meant when they talked about AI-assisted coding, so I used it (together with a great deal of my own editing and structuring) to help build the site you see now. What started as a technical curiosity quickly evolved into something more substantial — a free, non-commercial resource intended to help anyone interested in economic crime get a clearer picture of how it actually works and how the world is trying to fight it.

The hub brings together several layers:

🔍
Typologies & red-flag patterns — explanations of common economic crime typologies so people can recognise what they’re seeing
🕵️
Operations simulator — a simple simulator to let visitors walk through realistic detection and response scenarios
📋
Organisation directory — a growing directory of organisations, regulators, industry bodies, and specialist firms active in the economic crime space
◆
The Vault — a curated archive of books, documentaries, films, podcasts, reports, and other media that have shaped my own understanding of financial crime, money laundering, fraud, sanctions evasion, corruption, and the systems built to counter them

This is not meant to be an authoritative textbook, professional training platform, or official reference. It’s simply one person’s collection of explanations, tools, and resources that I’ve found useful — shared openly in the hope that students, early-career professionals, journalists, researchers, compliance practitioners, or anyone curious about economic crime might find value here too.

⚠ Important Notes

EconomicCrimeHub.org has absolutely no connection to my employer or any organisation I am or have been associated with. It is 100% my personal initiative, reflects only my own views and curation decisions, and is not endorsed by, affiliated with, or representative of any employer or other entity.

I’ve taken all steps I’m aware of to ensure the content and operation of the site comply with applicable laws, rules, and regulations concerning information sharing, copyright, data protection, and financial services communications.

Nothing on this site constitutes legal, regulatory, compliance, professional, or investment advice. Use the materials here as a starting point for your own research and learning — not as a substitute for qualified guidance.

What has surprised and inspired me most is how much I’ve learned and how energising the whole process has been. “Vibe coding” this site — letting ideas flow freely with AI help while shaping them into something coherent — has unlocked a ton of creativity for me.

As a result, I now have a long list of other ideas and features I plan to roll out in the coming days and weeks to make the hub even more useful and engaging.
